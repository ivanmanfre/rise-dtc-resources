# Content System — Walkthrough Video Script

**Format:** screen-recording walkthrough of the live dashboard + talking-head intro/outro
**Target length:** ~3 minutes (≈480 spoken words)
**Voice:** conversational creator register — warm, direct, a little informal. First person. No jargon, no node counts, no "315 workflows." Sell the outcome, show the proof.
**Screen path:** follow the in-app guided tour (`/dashboard-v2?tour=1`): Pipeline → Create → Edit → Schedule → Lead magnet → Performance.

> Recording note: open the dashboard, hit ▶ Tour (or load `?tour=1`), and let the six tour stops pace the screen capture. Narrate over each stop. Keep the cursor moving slowly; pause on each card.

---

## 0 · Cold open — talking head (0:00–0:15)

> [ON CAMERA]

Hey — quick one. Everyone tells you to post every day. Nobody tells you how to actually keep that up without it eating your whole week.

So I built a system that does it for me. Let me show you the real thing — this is my actual dashboard, nothing staged.

---

## 1 · The pipeline (0:15–0:40)

> [SCREEN: Content Studio → Posts, board view. Tour card: "Your content pipeline"]

This is a full week of content — already drafted, reviewed, and lined up to publish. I didn't write any of it.

Every card here started as an idea the system pulled in, turned into a post in my voice, and moved through review on its own. My job is basically to glance at it.

---

## 2 · Create — one line in (0:40–1:05)

> [SCREEN: "New post" form opens. Tour card: "Turn one line into a post"]

When I do want something specific, watch — I type one line. Just the idea.

That's it. The system writes the hook, the body, and the image — in my voice, not generic AI mush. It already knows how I write, what I believe, and the stuff I'd never say.

---

## 3 · Edit — you stay in control (1:05–1:35)

> [SCREEN: a draft opens in the editor. Tour card: "Edit anything before it ships"]

And nothing goes out without me. Every draft is quality-checked and voice-matched first — then I can open it and tweak anything.

Change a line, swap the image, move the timing. It's a draft I'm approving, not a black box I'm hoping works. That's the part people don't believe until they see it.

---

## 4 · Schedule — the feed never goes quiet (1:35–1:55)

> [SCREEN: Calendar. Tour card: "It schedules itself"]

Once I approve, it schedules itself — right into a steady rhythm.

This is the whole calendar, filling itself in. No more "oh no, I haven't posted in a week." The consistency just… happens.

---

## 5 · Lead magnet — attention becomes leads (1:55–2:25)

> [SCREEN: Lead Magnets, grid view with covers. Tour card: "The same idea captures leads"]

Here's where it stops being "just content." That same idea can also become a lead magnet — an interactive asset on a live page.

So a post doesn't just get likes. It captures the right people and qualifies them, automatically. Attention turns into actual pipeline.

---

## 6 · Performance — it learns (2:25–2:45)

> [SCREEN: Performance. Tour card: "And it learns what lands"]

And the loop closes here. Real LinkedIn results feed back in — what landed, what didn't.

So the system keeps getting sharper. It doubles down on what actually works for my audience, on its own.

---

## 7 · Close — talking head + CTA (2:45–3:00)

> [ON CAMERA]

That's the system. One idea in, a week of content out — posts, carousels, lead magnets, even video — all in my voice, all on autopilot. I approve, it does the rest.

If you want this running for you, there's a link below to grab 20 minutes with me. I'll show you exactly how it'd map to your business.

> [CTA card / lower-third: "Book a 20-min look → /start"]

### Beat checklist (the six promises)
1. It runs — and shows you a full pipeline you didn't write.
2. Never a blank page — one line becomes a finished post in your voice.
3. Never slop — quality-checked, voice-matched, and you approve.
4. Never goes quiet — it schedules itself into a rhythm.
5. Attention → qualified leads — the same idea becomes a lead magnet.
6. It learns — real performance feeds back into what gets posted next.
